Income Expense Asset Liability Summary

PERSONAL FINANCIAL STATEMENT

Income:

Total yearly earnings from all sources. Use: Budgeting, financial planning, loan applications.

Expense:

Total yearly expenditures. Use: Identify spending patterns, budget adjustments.

Assets:

Resources with economic value owned. Use: Wealth assessment, collateral for loans.

Liabilities:

Financial debts or obligations. Use: Debt management, financial strategy formulation.

Net Worth:

Assets minus liabilities. Use: Overall financial position, wealth tracking over time.

Disposable Income:

Income left after taxes and expenses. Use: Discretionary spending, savings, investments.

RATIOS

Debt to Income:

Total debt as a percentage of income. Use: Evaluate borrowing capacity, financial health. Below 0.36: This DTI ratio indicates a lower proportion of debt compared to income. 0.36 to 0.43: This DTI ratio reflects a moderate proportion of debt compared to income. Above 0.43: This DTI ratio indicates a higher proportion of debt compared to income.

Savings to Debt:

Savings as a percentage of debt. Use: Assess financial security, emergency fund sufficiency. Below 0.10: This ratio shows a lower proportion of savings compared to debt. 0.10 to 0.20: This ratio reflects a moderate proportion of savings compared to debt. Above 0.20: This ratio indicates a higher proportion of savings compared to debt.

Housing to Income:

Housing costs as a percentage of income. Use: Assess housing affordability, financial stress. Below 0.30: This ratio shows a lower proportion of housing costs compared to income. 0.30 to 0.40: This ratio reflects a moderate proportion of housing costs compared to income. Above 0.40: This ratio indicates a higher proportion of housing costs compared to income.

FIRE:

Financial Independence, Retire Early ratio; expenses covered by passive income. Use: Gauge progress towards financial independence, retirement planning.Above 0.25: This ratio shows passive income covering a significant portion of expenses. 0.15 to 0.25: This ratio reflects passive income covering a moderate portion of expenses. Below 0.15: This ratio indicates passive income covering a lower portion of expenses.

GOVERMENT OBLIGATIONS

Taxable Income:

Gross income subject to taxes. Use: Tax planning, understand tax liabilities.

Regional Tax Payable:

Tax owed to the regional government. Use: Budget for tax obligations, financial planning.

Subregional Tax Payable:

Tax owed to subregion government. Use: Budgeting for taxe obligations, financial planning.

Total Tax Payable:

Sum of all taxes owed. Use: Financial planning, avoid underpayment penalties.

Self Employed CPP Payable:

Canada Pension Plan contributions for self-employed individuals. Use: Retirement planning for self-employed.

Total Employment Insurance Payable:

Contributions towards Employment Insurance. Use: Ensure coverage for job loss.

Total CPP Payable:

General contributions to Canada Pension Plan. Use: Retirement planning. NOTE: your employer may have already deducted a portion of this total off your pay and remits it to the goverment on your behlaf

Employed CPP Payable:

Specific CPP contributions for employed individuals. Use: Retirement savings adjustment.
